Health & Safety Guidelines for Contractors

Purpose

This manual sets out Hindalco Industries Ltd (HIL) expectations in areas of Health and Safety (H&S) of all contractors of HIL. It aims to ensure that all contractors comply with HIL’s health and safety requirements, thereby creating a safe working environment.

Scope

The requirements outlined in this document apply to all HIL contractors and can be used as a guidance document for other works/services. This ensures consistent application of H&S standards across all projects and services.

Definitions and Acronyms

  • HIL: Hindalco Industries Ltd.
  • H&S: Health and Safety.
  • PPE: Personal Protective Equipment.
  • Contractor: Any person or firm engaged by HIL to carry out work.

General Requirements

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Contractors must ensure that their employees are equipped with and use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) at all times when on HIL premises. This includes helmets, gloves, safety glasses, and other necessary gear.

Emergency Procedures

Contractors must be familiar with and adhere to all emergency procedures as outlined by HIL. This includes knowing the location of emergency exits, fire extinguishers, and first aid stations.

Specific Requirements

Site Safety

Contractors are responsible for maintaining a safe working environment on site. This includes proper signage, barriers, and adherence to safety protocols to prevent accidents and injuries.

Equipment Safety

All equipment brought on site by contractors must be properly maintained and inspected regularly to ensure it is in safe working condition. Any defective equipment must be reported and repaired immediately.

Training

Contractors must ensure that all their employees are adequately trained in the tasks they are performing and are aware of HIL’s health and safety guidelines. Regular training sessions should be conducted to keep employees updated on safety practices.

Penalties

If the Contractor fails to take all due precautions to ensure the safety of its staff and labor and implementation at the site, HIL reserves the right to impose penalties. This may include financial penalties, suspension of work, or termination of the contract, depending on the severity of the violation.
